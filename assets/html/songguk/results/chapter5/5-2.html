RESUME

Songguk-ri sites are located near Puyo, middle-west region of south Korea. The latitude and longitude of the sites is 36°16′ north and 127°22′ east. The sites are scattered all over the 8hectares hillsides area, Songguk-ri village and its vicinity. The excavations were carried out for four seasons from 1975 to 1978. Through the excavations, following sites were found:19 dwelling sites, 3 burial urns, 1 stone-lined tomb, and 1 kiln site which belong to Bronze Age, and a number of burial urns, pit tombs, and stone tombs which belong to his-toric period. In the present volume, only the prehistoric sites are discussed. The others will be published separately in future.

1. Sites

a) Bronze Age Dwelling Sites

The plans are round, rectangular and some of them, irregular. All the plans have subterranean or semi-subterranean structure. There is a clear difference between the rectangular and round dwelling sites. The former shows an oval pit in the middle of the plan which has two post holes, while the latter has nothing. Dwelling sites produce many relics such as plain coarse potteries, red burnished potteries, stone polished daggers, tubular jades and burnt rices.

b) Burial Urns

All burial urns were single-pottery and stood upright in pit. One of them was surrounded with three small slabs and covered with one flat slab. The other two urns were buried in pits with no slab surrounding. However, they have a slab cover. On the bottom of two urns, there is a hole which is supposed to be used for drainage. Several scores of tubular jades were found in a burial urn.

c) Stone-lined Pit Tomb

It was built with stone slabs: several flat pieces were laid on the bottom, 4 or 5pieces of stone slabs constituted each of the side walls and 1 or 2 slabs made up the head and foot walls. Cover is an oval flat stone and is enough to cover the whole pit. The tomb which oriented to north contained 1 Liaoning style bronze dagger, 1 bronze chisel, 17 pieces of tubular jade made of Amazone stone, 2 comma-shaped jades, 1 stone dagger, and 11 stone arrow-heads which show deep relation with the Bronze culture in Liaoning area, north-eastern China.

d) Kiln Site

It was damaged too severely to trace the original structure, but the plan is round, and is supposed to be an open fire Kiln site. Much clods of burnt earth were found at this site. It produced lots of sherds of plain coarse potteries, red burnished potteries and a small stone polished dagger. This is the first example of prehistoric kiln site found in Korea.

2. Relics

a) Plain coarse Pottery

There are several types such as Songguk-ri type, bowl, deep bowl and plain pottery with foot. Paste contains some particles of quartz, felspar and sand, but some potteries are analyzed to be made of pure fine clay. Songguk-ri type is characterized by outward-curving mouth, swollen body and diminished flat bottom, which has not been found in Korean peninsula.

b) Red Burnished Pottery

It has also several types: Songguk-ri type, long-necked jar, bowl, deep bowl, and plain pottery with foot. Paste is usually composed with fine clay, but some of them is tempered with fine sand. Long-necked jar has a form of round flat bottom, swollen body curved with dull angle and outward-curving mouth, which is a new example from Bronze Age sites in Korea.

c) Blackish Pottery

Only two pieces were excavated. One is a piece from bottom, and the other is of body. The tempered clay is mixed with fine sand. Both side of the pottery are blackish, but not polished. The shape of pottery is unknown.

d) Stone Implements

Stone artifacts for practical use such as dagger, arrowhead, spearhead, semi-lunar knife, saddle quern, plane blade, whetstone, spindle whirl and grooved axe were excavated, while no fishing implement was found. Mostly, schist, shale, sandstone of sedimentary rocks were used. Slate, hornfels of metamorphic rocks were also used. Most of semi-lunar knife for harvesting is characterized by alternative uniface blade with only one exception. All arrowheads have tangs. A lot of whetstones used for grinding other stone tools indicated that Songguk-ri inhabitant could make all kind of stone tools for themselves. Many semi-lunar knives tell the arrival of the farming.

3. Others

There were two relics which were found for the first time from the dwelling site in southern part of Korean peninsula: one is a piece of burnt wooden plank (40×30×1cm thick) and the other, stone mould of fan-shaped bronze axe. A tubular jade was found under the plank. In Liaoning area, the fan-shaped bronze axe has usually been associated with the Liaoning type bronze dagger, and same type of dagger was found from the stone-lined pit tomb. The inhab-itants who lived in these dwelling sites, therefore, could be identified as the owners of the tomb.

Conclusion

1) The area amounting to 8 hectares where the sites are located would be the largest one which was formed during single cultural stage.

2) Among the plain coarse pottery, a unique shape was found, which has never been found from any other site. Hence Songguk-ri type plain coarse pottery.

3) Songguk-ri people Used the burial system such as stone-lined pit tomb and burial urn which were found near the dwelling sites.

4) Dominant finding of semi-lunar knives and burnt rice show that the inhab-itants were in the stage of rice-farming.

5) The stone mould of an axe means they already knew the casting of bronze implements and used them for practical use. The shape of mould including Liaoning type bronze dagger from stone-lined pit tomb shows the deep influence from the bronze culture of Liaoning region.

6) Chronologically, these sites could be dated to be bronze culture site between late 6th c. B. C. and early 4th c. B. C.
